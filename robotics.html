Past Developments In Machine Learning

Robotics is an interesting field, as a flashpoint in between the diverse worlds of technology and engineering. Over time, robots have gotten much more advanced, and are now incredibly intricate machines which are already revolutionizing the way we conduct our lives. From medicine to construction, the possibilities are endless.🤖

Feel free to check out this interesting article on the past of robotics.

As a substitute for human actions​, the development of robots must be done with the best intentions in mind, and subject to exhaustive tests, especially when a matter of life and death is in a machine's hand. Otherwise, the consequences may be DISASTROUS! Yet, the benefits are tremendous, and can prolong human life, eliminate back-breaking tasks in which labours suffer, and reduce human error. So far, it has been developed in this way. Or so we believe 👀! One can hope this is not the case.

Check out this excerpt from the JRank Science & Philosophy Blog!

"Robots have come to play a widespread and crucial role in many industrial operations today. These robots are almost always of the Jacquard type—with few human features—rather than the Jacquet-Droz, doll-like style. The work that robots do can be classified into three major categories: in the assembly and finishing of products; in the movement of materials and objects; and in the performance of work in environmentally difficult or hazardous situations. The most common single application of robots is in welding. About a quarter of all robots used by industry have this function"

Delving Into The Future

In the medical field, there is the possibility for advanced robotic arms to assist surgeons in even greater capacity then they do today. For example, would a robot be able to assess a patient, and then outline a treatment plan based on the certain characteristics it diagnoses, even going so far as to conduct the surgical procedure autonomously? Could this be done with little to NO human oversight? These are all questions we must ponder as robotics infiltrates into our daily lives, and brings with it an increasingly diverse range of skilled jobs, from programmers to design these wonders of man, to h​ardware specialists who choose specific materials in order to gain the best results🧪.



Tips & Tricks

  1. Make sure your robot is needed.

  2. Leverage open-source software.

  3. Start with the software, then build the robot.

  4. Simulation doesn’t compare to the real world.

  5. Cloud services are your friend.

  6. Be a part of the robotics community.
